## 1. Protocol Architecture Overview

Iris Protocol is a next-generation, high-performance DeFi ecosystem built around a dual-token mechanism and a multi-layered NFT infrastructure. The system features an advanced on-chain governance overlay ("Sovereign Kings") and an automated leveraged spot trading engine.

### The System Components:
1. **Underlying Asset:** Tether (USDT) - The foundational unit of account, liquidity, and collateral (6 Decimals).
2. **IXToken:** A yield-bearing, rebasing vault token wrapping the underlying USDT. Value accrues natively via share-to-asset conversion rates (`convertToShares` / `convertToAssets`).
3. **VotingEscrow:** The veToken locking contract where users escrow `IXToken` to gain voting weight based on block numbers.
4. **IrisGovernor:** The core governance brain executing community proposals through a Timelock.
5. **7KingsNFT:** An elite tier of 7 unique NFTs that grant absolute veto powers over the governance lifecycle.
6. **5KeeperNFT:** An exclusive collection of 5 NFTs acting as execution keys for protocol automation.
7. **Consoul:** The foundation revenue and protocol fee collection contract that distributes real yield to `7KingsNFT` holders.
8. **IXLeveragedSpotAdaptor:** The financial module enabling leveraged spot trading positions.

## 2. Tokenomics & Mathematical Accounting

### IXToken Rebasing Dynamics
The `IXToken` acts as a dynamic yield vault. When yield is generated by the underlying strategies, the pool assets increase while the total shares remain stable, meaning the value of each share grows over time:
* Deposit: Pulls `Asset (USDT)` $\rightarrow$ Mints `Shares (IXToken)`
* Withdrawal: Burns `Shares (IXToken)` $\rightarrow$ Payouts `Asset (USDT)` including accumulated yield.

### VotingEscrow (veToken) Mechanics
Unlike standard Curve-style veToken models, governance weight in Iris is determined by **locked rebasing share counts**, rather than time-decaying curves.
* **Anti-Vote Renting Enforcements:** 
  * Manual delegation is completely disabled. Both `delegate` and `delegateBySig` functions unconditionally `revert ManualDelegationNotAllowed()`.
  * One lock is allowed per address. `delegates(account)` always returns `account` itself (implicit automatic self-delegation upon locking assets).
* **The Block-Clock (ERC-6372):**
  * All checkpoints, voting power lookups, and total supply metrics are keyed by **`block.number`** (`mode=blocknumber&from=default`).
  * Unit of measure conversions (assuming 12-second Ethereum block-times):
    * `MIN_LOCK_DURATION` = 50,400 blocks (~1 week)
    * `MAX_LOCK_DURATION` = 10,512,000 blocks (~4 years)

## 3. Core Governance Specification (IrisGovernor)

The `IrisGovernor` contract is built using OpenZeppelin V5 extensions, fully customized to operate on block numbers and handle the Sovereign Kings elite layer.

### System Parameters:
* **Contract Name:** "Iris Governor"
* **Voting Delay:** 21,600 blocks (~3 days) — Period between proposal submission and voting start.
* **Voting Period:** 151,200 blocks (~3 weeks) — Active voting window.
* **Proposal Threshold:** 1,000 voting units ($1,000 \times 10^6$ accounting for underlying asset's 6 decimals).
* **Quorum Fraction:** 10% of the total voting supply at the proposal snapshot block.
* **Timelock Controller:** All passed proposals must route through an OpenZeppelin `TimelockController` to undergo a quarantine period before physical execution.

### Native State Injection
To accommodate the custom veto structures, the contract overrides OpenZeppelin's native `state` function. If a proposal is flagged as vetoed, the system halts immediately:

$$ProposalState = \begin{cases} Canceled, & \text{if } isVetoed[proposalId] == true \\ super.state(proposalId), & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

## 4. The Elite Overlay: Sovereign Kings Game Theory

The most innovative feature of Iris Governance is the dual-layered veto layer controlled exclusively by holders of the **7 Kings NFTs**. This system creates an intense game-theoretic dynamic between diplomatic consensus and individual sacrifice.

### Mechanism 1: Diplomatic Council Veto (`councilVeto` + `councilExecute`)
* **Objective:** Allows the Kings to collaborate and stop a malicious or suboptimal proposal that passed community voting.
* **Workflow:** Kings register their opposition by calling `councilVeto(proposalId, tokenId)`. This records their alignment in an isolated array (`_councilVetoers`).
* **Execution Window:** Active during both `Active` (community voting) and `Queued` (Timelock execution quarantine) states, providing an ultimate backstop against late-stage governance exploits.
* **The Threshold Formula:** Any active King can call `councilExecute`. The system loops through the voters and counts only those who **still hold their King NFT** (live balance checking). The veto executes successfully if the live voter count exceeds the absolute majority of the current total supply:
  $$\text{validCouncilVetoers} > \frac{\text{kingsNFT.totalSupply()}}{2}$$
* **Economic Cost:** **No NFTs are burned.** Power is derived purely through collective diplomatic consensus.

### Mechanism 2: Sacrificial Kamikaze Veto (`aKingForAllVeto`)
* **Objective:** A nuclear option bypass used when a critical threat is imminent and council consensus cannot be achieved in time.
* **Workflow:** Any individual King can unilaterally call `aKingForAllVeto(proposalId, tokenId)`.
* **Execution:** The contract **instantly and permanently burns** the caller's King NFT (`kingsNFT.burn(tokenId)`), bypasses all other voting metrics, and aggressively shifts `isVetoed[proposalId]` to `true`.
* **The Supply Deflation Loop:** Burning an NFT permanently decreases `kingsNFT.totalSupply()` (e.g., from 7 down to 6). This dynamically alters the absolute majority calculation for all future council vetoes.
* **System Guardrail:** To prevent complete extinction of the elite layer, the burn will fail if it is the last token remaining: `if (totalSupply == 1) revert TheLastKingShouldSurvive()`.

### The Consoul Engine & Real Yield Incentive
The **Consoul** contract collects protocol revenue (surplus foundation capital and `IXLeveragedSpotAdaptor` trading/performance fees) and distributes it directly to the active holders of the `7KingsNFT`.
* **The Game-Theoretic Trap:** This creates a severe financial penalty for using the *Kamikaze Veto*. A King who executes a solo veto destroys their token, forever relinquishing their lifelong share of protocol dividends. Therefore, a King will only activate the nuclear option if the proposal poses an existential threat to the entire protocol's survival.

## 5. The Automation & Trading Layer (IXLeveragedSpotAdaptor & Keepers)

### Capital Efficiency & Liquidity Interactivity
The `IXLeveragedSpotAdaptor` functions as the financial execution core. It interacts natively with the `IXToken` pool to source underlying asset liquidity (USDT) for opening high-efficiency leveraged spot trading positions. Fees generated from spreads, trade openings, and liquidations flow directly into the `Consoul` contract.

### 5 Keeper NFTs
The financial and trading operations inside the `IXLeveragedSpotAdaptor` require continuous off-chain monitoring and automated triggers (e.g., liquidation triggers, position health adjustments, oracle pings).
* **Role:** These critical operations are restricted via custom modifiers ensuring access is granted only to holders of the **5 Keeper NFTs**.
* **Utility:** These NFTs function as cryptographic authorization keys for automated bot infrastructure. By limiting keeper capabilities to exactly 5 tokens, the protocol minimizes its attack surface, prevents front-running/MEV bot exploitation on liquidations, and guarantees operational security.
